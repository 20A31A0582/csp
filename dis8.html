Rice Blast

Symptoms:

->Infections can kill the entire leaf and may
extend a few millimeters into and around the sheath
->The fungus may produce spores on these lesions
->Rice necks and panicles
->The neck of the rice plant refers to that portion of the stem that rises above the leaves and supports the seed head or panicle


Management:

Rice blast can be controlled by a combination of preventive measures and
foliar fungicides applied when rice is in the late boot stage and again when it is 80 to 90 percent headed
->Seed treatment at 2.0 g/kg seed with Captan or Carbendazim or Thiram or Tricyclazole.
->Spraying of Tricyclazole at 1g/lit of water or Edifenphos at 1 ml/lit of water or Carbendazim at 1.0 gm/lit.
